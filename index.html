Meet Thabo: A Rural Learner

Our story follows Thabo, a bright student in a rural village facing challenges accessing consistent electricity and internet for digital education. Through his character, we explore the barriers and demonstrate how our architecture aims to overcome them, ensuring inclusivity.

Thabo's Learning Journey

Follow Thabo's path from limited connectivity to engaging fully with digital learning resources. This journey illustrates how different components of the architecture support inclusive access at each stage:

  • Discovery: Finding relevant educational resources via a mobile-friendly portal, even with low bandwidth.
  • Access: Downloading content offline during brief connectivity periods at a community hub or school.
  • Engagement: Participating in offline activities, syncing progress later, and joining asynchronous discussions.
  • Mastery: Submitting assignments and taking assessments designed for low-bandwidth or offline completion.

Narrative Frameworks

Storytelling enhances engagement. Our framework utilizes narratives to create meaningful connections:

  • Personal Journeys: Highlighting diverse student and teacher experiences like Thabo's.
  • Problem-Solving Scenarios: Engaging learners with locally relevant challenges requiring critical thinking.
  • Interactive Storylines: Adapting content delivery based on user progress and preferences.
  • Community Narratives: Connecting learning outcomes to real-world applications within the local context.

More examples and implementation guidelines are available in the full framework documentation.
